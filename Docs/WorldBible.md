# World Bible

## World Name

Working name: **Dracoria**

## Tone

Stylized high-fantasy MMO world with warmth, danger, wonder, and player-driven civilization. The target feeling is classic MMO adventure: readable, colorful, inviting, and alive.

## Visual Direction

Initial style target:

- Classic WoW-inspired stylized 3D
- Lower poly than modern AAA games
- Strong silhouettes
- Bold color palettes
- Readable characters and buildings on mobile screens
- Cozy but adventurous fantasy tone
- RuneScape-like life-skill readability and progression clarity

The art should not chase photorealism. It should be durable, charming, performant, and expandable.

## Creation Myth

Long before the current kingdoms, the world was bound by an unseen force called **The Weave**.

The Weave is not a god. It is the binding force of reality: land, sea, magic, memory, time, and life.

At some point in the ancient past, the Weave fractured. It did not fully break, but the fractures caused strange changes across the world:

- Living forests
- Elementals
- Floating ruins
- Sea monsters
- Ancient machines
- Magical storms
- Lost islands
- Forgotten roads

The world is not dying. It is changing.

## The First Civilization

Every current race believes they were among the first to shape the world.

They are all wrong.

Before Humans, Elves, Orcs, Goblins, and Dragonborn, there was an unknown civilization. Their roads, ruins, temples, underwater cities, and sky structures still exist. Players slowly uncover their history over years.

This gives the game permanent mystery without needing one main villain.

## The Five Great Threats

The game should rarely rely on one dark lord. Instead, civilization faces forces:

1. **The Wild**: Nature reclaiming civilization.
2. **The Deep**: Ocean horrors, lost kingdoms, abyssal mysteries.
3. **The Shattered**: Reality fractures, magical anomalies, unstable regions.
4. **The Forgotten**: Ancient civilizations and sleeping powers returning.
5. **Ourselves**: Greed, politics, piracy, war, betrayal, and ambition.

## The Crown Isles

A central contested island chain where weekly kingdom campaigns take place.

Purpose:

- Keeps large-scale war away from peaceful heartlands.
- Gives competitive players a meaningful battlefield.
- Lets every kingdom fight for weekly world blessings.
- Connects sailing, supply lines, naval combat, crafting, cooking, scouting, logistics, and warfare.

Lore:

Long ago, every kingdom tried to claim the Crown Isles. None could hold them forever. A treaty was formed: the Isles belong to whoever can hold and power the Crown Beacon.

Victory condition:

The winning kingdom controls and powers the Crown Beacon at the end of the campaign.

The Beacon requires:

- Supplies
- Food
- Weapons
- Repairs
- Runes
- Fuel
- Ships
- Siege equipment
- Fighters
- Healers
- Scouts
- Crafters

Rewards should be called **Kingdom Blessings**, not just server buffs.

Examples:

- Blessing of the Harvest
- Blessing of the Forge
- Blessing of the Tide
- Blessing of Discovery
- Blessing of Commerce

- Open Sea: trade, fishing, storms, merchant fleets.
- Wild Seas: pirates, sea monsters, ghost ships, ruins.
- The Abyss: dangerous deep ocean mysteries.

The sea supports:

- Fishing
- Shipping
- Piracy
- Trade
- Navigation
- Exploration

- Island discovery
- Naval warfare
- Passenger ferry services

## Travel Philosophy

> Distance creates stories.

Travel is gameplay. Players can travel by foot, mount, caravan, ferry, player ship, guild convoy, or kingdom transport.

Early game must include NPC ships, ferries, caravans, and stagecoaches so players can reach far places before player-run services mature.

NPC transport is safe, cheap, fixed-route, and slow.
Player transport is faster, flexible, social, profitable, and more prestigious.

## Server History

Every server writes its own Chronicle.

The Chronicle records:

- First city founded
- First ship crossing
- First legendary item
- First sea monster defeated
- Famous guild wars
- Major famines
- Major festivals
- World projects
- Kingdom victories
- Family dynasties

A player joining years later should feel like they arrived in a world with history, not like they missed the game.
