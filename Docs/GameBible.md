# Dracoria Expanse Game Bible

## Working Title

**Dracoria Expanse**

## Genre

Mobile-first living fantasy MMO with stylized classic MMO visuals, player-driven economy, professions, sailing, guilds, dynasties, communities, player cities, faction campaigns, and long-term world history.

## Core Promise

> Live a life worth remembering.

The player is not forced to become only a warrior. They may become a fisherman, ship captain, blacksmith, mayor, merchant, navigator, guild leader, explorer, pirate, farmer, cook, caravan guard, or founder of a family dynasty.

## North Star

> Build a living fantasy world where every player can become legendary, regardless of how they choose to live.

## The Game Is About

- Building a life, not rushing to max level.
- Creating civilization, not simply consuming content.
- Letting every profession matter.
- Letting players rely on each other.
- Letting servers create their own histories.
- Making travel, trade, work, war, and community all part of the adventure.

## The Game Is Not About

- A single chosen-one story.
- Everyone chasing the same combat endgame.
- Instant teleport convenience replacing world travel.
- Global auction houses replacing local markets.
- Crafting as a side menu.
- Cities as decoration only.
- Professions that become obsolete.

## The Five Pillars

### 1. Live Anywhere

Players begin as travelers and choose their life through actions. They may live in a shack, a homestead, a community village, a guild city, a port, a family estate, or aboard a ship.

### 2. Build Together

Communities become villages. Villages feed cities. Cities shape kingdoms. Kingdoms shape history.

### 3. Every Profession Matters

Every profession has progression, bosses, mastery, reputation, rare discoveries, special equipment, and legendary opportunities.

### 4. The World Evolves

Seasons change. Weather affects travel. Markets fluctuate. Animals migrate. Roads become famous. Cities age. Ships gain histories. Families become known.

### 5. Legacy

The highest achievement is not simply power. It is leaving something behind that the world remembers.

## Player Identity

Players do not choose a permanent class. They choose race, appearance, homeland, family name, and then become who they are through action.

The first thing another player should see is not only a level number. They should see identity:

```text
Corey Kranert
House Kranert
Human
Navigator, Fisherman, Blacksmith
Home: Ironhaven
Known For: Builder of the Great Fleet
Guild: Wolfpack
Reputation: Honored
```

## Family and Dynasty System

Instead of deleting old characters, players build a family line.

- The account creates a House name, crest, motto, and heritage.
- Characters belong to that House.
- Older characters can ascend to Elder status.
- Elders remain in the world as mentors, shopkeepers, retired captains, or estate NPCs.
- Family homes become estates and compounds over time.
- Family reputation opens doors but does not replace effort.
- Family heirlooms carry history more than raw power.

## Communities

Not every player wants a guild city. Small groups can form communities.

Civilization ladder:

```text

Village
Guild
Guild Settlement
Town
City

```

Small friend groups can create villages with shared roads, docks, markets, farms, taverns, and community halls.

## First 24 Hours Experience

The first day should make the player feel like they arrived in a real place.

- Character creation has no class selection.
- Player starts in a frontier village.
- They receive basic survival tools, not just a sword.
- Early choices introduce fishing, blacksmithing, gathering, delivery, exploration, and guard duty.
- First city visit should feel alive with players, markets, docks, NPC routines, guild recruitment, and trade.
- First meaningful purchase should ideally come from another player.
- First boat trip happens within the first day.
- First small achievement is recorded in the Legacy Journal.

## MVP Target

The first prototype should prove the feeling, not the full scope.

Minimum Magical Product:

- One starter region
- Ironhaven village/harbor
- Third-person movement
- Mobile controls
- Character data
- Family name data
- Skills
- Inventory
- Fishing
- Basic combat
- Basic NPC dialogue
- Basic save/load
- One marketplace stub
- One ship/travel stub
- One community/homestead stub

## Development Rule

Build one expandable slice at a time. Do not attempt the full MMO before the prototype feels good.
